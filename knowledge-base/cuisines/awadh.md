## Overview

Awadh, centred on Lucknow, is famous for its **refined, courtly cuisine**. The nawabs patronised elaborate cooking, giving rise to **dum pukht** (sealed, slow steam-cooking), silk-smooth kebabs like **galouti** and **kakori**, and aromatic kormas and pulaos.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: onion, ginger, garlic, green cardamom, clove, cinnamon, bay leaf, saffron.
- **Signature ingredients**: raw papaya (tenderizer), nuts (almond, cashew), milk, cream, silver leaf.
- **Spices**: mild chilli, aromatic garam masala, saffron.
- **Fats**: ghee, butter, meat fat.
- **Souring**: very little; richness and aroma dominate.

## Techniques

- **Dum pukht**: rice and meat sealed in a heavy pot with dough and slow-cooked.
- **Fine kebabs**: meat triple-ground, tenderised with raw papaya, cooked gently to “melt in the mouth”.
- **Kormas**: onion–nut pastes, yoghurt/cream, saffron, mild spices.
- **Elegant pulaos**: aromatic rice cooked in stock, garnished with silver leaf.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: gentle kormas, galouti-style ground patties, dum-cooked fish with rice.
- **Venison**: galouti or kakori-style kebabs, dum braises.
- **Wild boar**: nut-based korma, slow-cooked dum dishes.
- **Duck/goose**: rich kormas and pulaos.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Dum base**: sautéed onion + aromatics in ghee, add protein, yoghurt/nut paste, rice (if using), seal with dough and cook low and slow.
- **Awadhi korma**: onion–nut paste, ghee, spices, yoghurt/cream, saffron finish.
