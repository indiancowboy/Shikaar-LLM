## Overview

Agra and Delhi were major Mughal imperial cities, with cuisines built around **courtly kitchens, kebabs, kormas, and biryanis**. Rich gravies, nuts, dried fruit, and layered spices define this food, alongside vibrant street snacks like **chaat**.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: onion, ginger, garlic, green chilli, tomato.
- **Signature ingredients**: nuts (almond, cashew), dried fruit, saffron, rice.
- **Spices**: cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, bay leaf, cumin, coriander, chilli.
- **Fats**: ghee, oil, meat fat.
- **Souring**: yoghurt, tomato.

## Techniques

- **Korma cooking**: onion + ginger-garlic + aromatic spices, finished with yoghurt or cream.
- **Kebabs**: skewered or griddled, more robust than Awadhi kebabs, e.g. barra kebab.
- **Biryani & pulao**: layered rice and meat with saffron and whole spices.
- **Street chaat**: aloo tikki, pani puri, tangy chutneys and crisp textures.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: korma-style fish, light biryani, pan-fried fish kebabs.
- **Venison**: rich korma, do-pyaza style curry, griddled kebabs.
- **Wild boar**: tomato-onion Mughal gravies, ground meat kebabs, biryani.
- **Duck/goose**: luxurious kormas and biryanis.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Mughal korma base**: browned onions, ginger-garlic, whole spices, yoghurt/cream, saffron.
- **Delhi kebab marinade**: ground or chunked meat with onion, ginger-garlic, chilli, garam masala, fat → skewered and grilled.
