## Overview

Kerala, a lush spice-growing coastal state, is defined by **coconut, pepper, curry leaves, and seafood**. Arab, Jewish, Syrian Christian, and Portuguese influences layer over a strong local tradition, producing famous coconut milk fish curries, backwater prawn and crab dishes, and Malabar biryanis.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: curry leaves, ginger, garlic, green and dried red chillies, black pepper, turmeric.
- **Signature ingredients**: coconut oil, coconut milk, grated coconut, kodampuli (smoked tamarind), rice forms (appam, puttu, pathiri).
- **Spices**: mustard seeds, fenugreek, pepper, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom.
- **Fats**: coconut oil (primary), ghee for festive and Christian/Muslim dishes.
- **Souring**: tamarind, kodampuli, vinegar in some Christian dishes.

## Techniques

- **Coconut milk curries** for fish and vegetables.
- **Sour fish curries** with tamarind or kodampuli.
- **Pepper fry** in coconut oil with curry leaves.
- **Malabar biryani** with coconut, spices, and fried shallots.
- **Syrian Christian stews** with coconut milk and sometimes vinegar.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish (any)**: coconut milk curries, kodampuli-based sour curries, pepper-curry leaf fry.
- **Venison**: pepper fry, coconut-chilli curries.
- **Wild boar**: coconut-rich, peppery braises or sour curries.
- **Duck/goose**: coconut milk stews, peppery Christian-style dishes.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Moilee-style coconut curry**: coconut oil, ginger, garlic, curry leaves, green chilli, turmeric → add fish and coconut milk, simmer gently.
- **Kodampuli sour curry**: mustard + fenugreek temper, dried chillies, turmeric, kodampuli water → simmer fish.

- User has **fish, duck, venison, boar** and wants **coconut + spice**.
