## Overview

Bengal’s food is famous for **fish, mustard, and sweets**. Rivers and fertile land drive a fish-first cuisine balanced by the Ayurvedic six tastes, especially bitterness (tikta) and gentle sweetness. Mustard oil and mustard paste are defining flavours, alongside **panch phoron**, the crackling five-spice mix.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: mustard seed/paste, ginger, garlic, green chilli, turmeric.
- **Signature ingredients**: panch phoron (mustard, nigella, fenugreek, fennel, cumin), mustard oil, hilsa (as archetype for oily fish), chhena for sweets.
- **Spices**: mustard, nigella, fenugreek, turmeric, chilli.
- **Fats**: mustard oil (primary), ghee for sweets.
- **Souring**: tamarind, tomato, green mango, mustard-based tartness.

## Techniques

- **Mustard-based fish curries (shorshe)** using mustard paste and mustard oil.
- **Panch phoron tempering**: blooming the five-spice mix in hot oil.
- **Light frying in mustard oil** for fish and game.
- **Street snacks** (chops, kathi rolls, singara) built on frying and spice layering.
- **Sweets** with chhena and seasonal jaggery.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **White fish**: shorshe-style mustard curry, mustard oil pan-fry, panch phoron tempered gravies.
- **Oily fish**: hilsa-style marinades, pineapple-mustard curries, robust mustard-chilli preps.
- **Venison**: mustard-garlic fry, panch phoron stews.
- **Wild boar**: mustard-chilli braise with tamarind or tomato.
- **Duck/goose**: mustard-rich, slightly sweet-sour gravies.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Shorshe base**: mustard paste, mustard oil, turmeric, green chilli, salt → gentle simmer with fish/game.
- **Panch phoron temper**: crackle panch phoron in mustard oil, add protein + turmeric and cook.
