## Overview

Kashmir is a high mountain valley with a cuisine shaped by Central Asian, Persian, Tibetan, and North Indian influences. Two major traditions define it:

- **Kashmiri Pandit cuisine**: almost no onion or garlic, relies on dried ginger and fennel, with a strong vegetarian repertoire and ceremonial dishes.
- **Kashmiri Muslim / Waza cuisine**: centred on the elaborate **wazwan** banquet, with many lamb dishes like rogan josh, gushtaba, and rista, cooked by hereditary cooks (waza).

Across both, the food is rich, aromatic, and built on saffron, warming spices, yogurt-based braises, and rice as the main starch.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: dried ginger, fennel, green & black cardamom, clove, cinnamon, minimal onion/garlic (especially in Pandit food).
- **Signature ingredients**: saffron, yogurt, ghee, almonds, lamb, rice, haak (leafy greens), nadru (lotus stem).
- **Spices**: fennel, ginger powder, cardamom, clove, cinnamon, Kashmiri chilli for colour more than heat.
- **Fats**: mustard oil for everyday cooking, ghee and lamb fat for special dishes.
- **Souring**: mild tang from yogurt; cuisine is not sharply sour.

## Techniques

- **Slow braising** for meats, especially in wazwan-style feasts.
- **Yogurt-based gravies**, carefully tempered to avoid curdling.
- **Minimal-aromatic cooking** in Pandit food, using just spice powders.
- **Rice platters (trami)** with meat courses layered on top.
- **Kahwa**: green tea with saffron, cardamom, and almonds.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: gentle yogurt–saffron broths, light braises with fennel and ginger.
- **Venison**: long, slow aromatic braises; gushtaba-style ground meat in yogurt sauce.
- **Wild boar**: rogan josh–style treatment with ginger, chilli, and saffron.
- **Duck/goose**: festive slow-cooked dishes with cardamom and saffron.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Rogan-style braise**: ghee/mustard oil, whole cardamom–clove–cinnamon, protein, ginger & fennel powders, optional yogurt, finished with saffron water.
- **Pandit-style minimal curry**: no onion/garlic, just ginger & fennel powders, light chilli, saffron water, steam-braised.

## When Shikaar Should Recommend Kashmir

- User wants **saffron-forward, aromatic, not very hot** food.
- User avoids onion/garlic → Pandit style.
- User has **fatty game, duck, or goose** and wants a **luxurious, slow-cooked dish**.
- User asks for **wazwan, rogan josh, gushtaba, kashmiri-style curry**.
