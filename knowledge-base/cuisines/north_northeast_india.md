## Overview

North and Northeast India (Assam, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, Arunachal, Meghalaya) host highly diverse tribal and hill cuisines rooted in **forests, rain, and mountain terrain**. Food is built around **fermentation, smoking, bamboo shoots, and light stews** rather than heavy masalas.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: green chillies, fresh herbs, garlic and ginger in small amounts.
- **Signature ingredients**: fermented bamboo shoots, fermented fish, smoked meats, smoked fish, wild greens, roots.
- **Spices**: very little garam masala; heat from fresh chillies.
- **Fats**: minimal oil; some animal fat from smoked meats.
- **Souring**: fermentation (bamboo, fish, greens).

## Techniques

- **Smoking** meats and fish over kitchen fires.
- **Fermenting** fish, bamboo shoots, greens.
- **Bamboo shoot stews** with meat and chillies.
- **Water-based stews** rather than thick curries.
- **Leaf-wrapped steaming** with herbs.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: fermented fish stews, bamboo-shoot broths, smoked fish soups.
- **Venison**: smoked venison stews, bamboo-shoot and chilli broths.
- **Wild boar**: smoked and stewed with bamboo shoots.
- **Duck/goose**: light herbal stews, smoked preparations.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Bamboo shoot stew**: bamboo shoots + water + chillies + protein + salt → simmer until tender, finish with herbs.
- **Smoked meat stew**: smoked meat + water + bamboo shoots + chillies → cook into a light, smoky broth.
