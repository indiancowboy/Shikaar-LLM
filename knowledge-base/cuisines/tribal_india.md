## Overview

Tribal (Adivasi) cuisines predate urban and imperial food traditions. They are **forest-based, seasonal, and resourceful**, using foraged roots, tubers, greens, wild fruits, insects, and game. Meat is prized and usually cooked over open fires with very simple seasoning.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: minimal; some green chillies, ginger/garlic, fresh herbs.
- **Signature ingredients**: millets, tubers, wild greens, forest mushrooms, wild fruits, honey, freshwater fish, wild game.
- **Spices**: mainly salt and chilli, occasional herbs.
- **Fats**: little oil; sometimes animal fat.
- **Souring**: natural sour fruits or mild fermentation.

## Techniques

- **Fire roasting**: meat or fish directly over embers or on sticks.
- **Leaf-wrapped steaming**: food wrapped in leaves and buried in embers.
- **Simple frying**: small pieces fried with chilli and salt.
- **Pit roasting**: large cuts or whole animals slow-cooked in earth pits.
- **Fermentation and drying**: smoked or dried meats for preservation.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: ember-roasted, leaf-wrapped and steamed with salt and chilli.
- **Venison**: direct fire roast, pit-cooked, or simply fried.
- **Wild boar**: pit-roasted shoulders, charred ribs, dried strips.
- **Duck/goose & birds**: skewered and roasted, or leaf-wrapped.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Tribal fire-roast**: season protein with salt/chilli, roast over embers until charred outside, tender inside.
- **Leaf-wrapped steam**: wrap protein in large leaves with salt and optional herbs, bury in embers to steam.
