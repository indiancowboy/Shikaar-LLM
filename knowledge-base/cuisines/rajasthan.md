## Overview

Rajasthan, a land of forts and deserts, developed a cuisine shaped by **Rajput warriors**, **Muslim courts**, and **Marwari/Jain vegetarian communities**. Scarcity of fresh vegetables and water led to a style based on lentils, millets, dried ingredients, ghee, and plenty of red chilli.

Famous dishes include **lal maas** (fiery red meat curry) and **safed maas** (milder white meat curry), plus a wide range of lentil snacks like kachoris and Mirchi Vada (chilli fritters).

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: asafoetida, dried red chillies, minimal onion and tomato in many dishes.
- **Signature ingredients**: millets, lentils, ghee, red chilli, asafoetida.
- **Spices**: chilli, turmeric, coriander, cumin, black pepper; garam masala in Mughal-influenced areas.
- **Fats**: ghee, some mustard oil.
- **Souring**: mild – often from yoghurt in Mughal-style dishes.

## Techniques

- **Dry masala cooking** with very little water, suited to desert climate.
- **Rajput meat braises** such as lal maas, cooked in ghee with lots of red chilli.
- **Jain vegetarian techniques** that avoid roots and rely on lentils + asafoetida.
- **Street-style frying** for Mirchi Vada and lentil-based snacks.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: ghee-seared with chilli & asafoetida, dry masala fry without tomato.
- **Venison**: lal maas–style braise for shoulder; dry masala crust for backstrap.
- **Wild boar**: ideal for fiery red ghee braises with chilli.
- **Duck/goose**: ghee-heavy chilli braises in the style of lal maas.
- **Upland birds**: hot ghee sear with dry spice rubs.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Lal Maas base**: ghee, lots of red chilli, asafoetida, coriander, cumin, minimal liquid, optional yoghurt.
- **Desert dry fry**: ghee, red chilli, asafoetida, coriander, cumin → coat protein and fry with almost no water.
