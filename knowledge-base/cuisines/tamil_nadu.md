## Overview

Tamil Nadu’s cuisine is built on **rice, lentils, pepper, and tamarind**, with powerful regional styles like **Chettinad**. Idli and dosa originate here, and temple and vegetarian food traditions are strong, but the region also has fiery non-veg cooking influenced by trade across Southeast Asia.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: black pepper, dried red chillies, curry leaves, mustard seeds, garlic, ginger, fennel (Chettinad), star anise, kalpasi.
- **Signature ingredients**: tamarind, sambar/rasam powders, fermented rice–lentil batters, dry coconut in Chettinad.
- **Spices**: black pepper, chilli, fennel, cinnamon, clove, star anise.
- **Fats**: gingelly (sesame) oil, coconut oil, ghee (temple/festive).
- **Souring**: tamarind, tomato, some lemon.

## Techniques

- **Pepper fry**: coarse black pepper with curry leaves and garlic, used for meats and fish.
- **Sambar & rasam**: tamarind-based lentil broths with spices and herbs.
- **Chettinad masala grinding**: roasted coconut + chilli + fennel + whole spices, ground to a paste.
- **Kothu-style stir fry**: chopped parotta or meat on a hot tawa with masala.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: pepper-tamarind fry, rasam-poached, Chettinad fish curry.
- **Venison**: pepper fry, Chettinad masala, tamarind curry.
- **Wild boar**: black-pepper-heavy Chettinad curry, tamarind braise.
- **Duck/goose**: tamarind-rich curries, pepper roasts.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Tamil pepper fry**: oil, fennel + curry leaves, garlic/ginger, coarse black pepper, protein → fry until coated.
- **Rasam-poached fish**: tamarind water, tomato, pepper, mustard, curry leaves → simmer; add fish to poach.
