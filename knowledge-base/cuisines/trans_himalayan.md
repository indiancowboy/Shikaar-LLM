## Overview

The Trans-Himalayan region (e.g. Ladakh) is a high, cold landscape with limited agriculture. Food is built for survival and warmth, with **barley and buckwheat**, **yak milk and butter**, and **simple meat stews** forming the core diet, alongside butter tea and dense flatbreads.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: very little; some ginger, garlic, chillies.
- **Signature ingredients**: barley, buckwheat, maize, yak milk, yak butter, cheese.
- **Spices**: mostly salt; small amounts of chilli and ginger.
- **Fats**: yak butter and animal fat.
- **Souring**: some fermentation tang; not strongly sour.

## Techniques

- **Simple stewing** of meat with grain and water.
- **Barley and buckwheat flatbreads** cooked on griddles.
- **Butter tea** made from yak butter, tea, salt.
- **Air- and smoke-drying** of meats and cheese.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: pan-seared in butter, or added to light ginger broths.
- **Venison/boar**: thick, warming stews with barley or buckwheat.
- **Duck/goose**: fatty braises in simple, lightly spiced broths.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Mountain meat stew**: meat + water + barley + salt + ginger, simmered until thick and warming, finished with butter.
- **Yak-butter sear**: gently sear fish or meat in butter with a little salt.
