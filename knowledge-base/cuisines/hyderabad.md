## Overview

Hyderabad’s cuisine, shaped by the Nizams, is one of India’s most opulent. It fuses **Arab, Persian, Turkish, Mughal, and South Indian** influences. Signature dishes include **biryani**, **haleem**, and a wide range of tangy, chilli-laced curries that use tamarind, raw mango, grapes, or pomegranate for sourness.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: onion, ginger, garlic, green chillies, curry leaves.
- **Signature ingredients**: kid/goat meat, tamarind, raw mango, unripe grapes, pomegranate, coconut, peanuts, sesame seeds.
- **Spices**: cumin, mustard, curry leaves, dried chilli, turmeric, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom.
- **Fats**: ghee, meat fat, oil.
- **Souring**: tamarind, lemon, mango, grapes, pomegranate.

## Techniques

- **Hyderabadi biryani**: marinated meat layered with par-cooked rice, fried onions, ghee, herbs, cooked on dum.
- **Haleem**: slow-cooked meat and cereal porridge, enriched with ghee.
- **Tangy curries** using tamarind, mango, or grapes.
- **Coconut–peanut–sesame gravies** blending North and South techniques.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **Fish**: tamarind or mango-based curries, biryani-style fish, coconut–peanut–sesame gravies.
- **Venison**: biryanis, tangy braises with tamarind, haleem-style porridges.
- **Wild boar**: chilli–tamarind braises, rich biryanis.
- **Duck/goose**: Nizami biryani or coconut–sesame stews.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Tamarind-mango masala**: mustard + curry leaves, onion, ginger-garlic, chilli, tamarind/raw mango → simmer with protein.
- **Hyderabadi biryani layering**: marinated meat, parboiled rice, fried onions, ghee, herbs, dum cooking.
