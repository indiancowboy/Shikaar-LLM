## Overview

The Western Coast—Goa, Konkan, and neighbouring shores—combines **Arab trade, Portuguese rule, and coastal Hindu/Muslim/Christian traditions**. Coconut, tamarind, kokum, and toddy vinegar underpin bold, sour-spicy curries and iconic dishes like **vindalho**, **xacuti**, and coastal fish fries.

## Core Flavor Building Blocks

- **Aromatics**: coconut (fresh, toasted, milk), tamarind, kokum, green and dried chillies, curry leaves, garlic, ginger, onions (esp. Goan Catholic).
- **Signature ingredients**: toddy/palm vinegar, kokum (donkey plum), xacuti masala, Goan sausages.
- **Spices**: dried red chillies, pepper, cloves, cinnamon, mustard seeds, turmeric, coriander, cumin, fenugreek.
- **Fats**: coconut oil, some ghee.
- **Souring**: tamarind, kokum, toddy vinegar.

## Techniques

- **Vindalho**: garlic–chilli–vinegar–tamarind paste used to marinate and cook meats or fish.
- **Xacuti**: roasted coconut and chilli-based masala for thick, complex curries.
- **Coconut-chilli curries** for fish and game.
- **Kokum-soured broths** and curries for coastal fish.

## Protein Pairing (Shikaar)

- **White fish**: kokum or tamarind curries, Konkan coconut-chilli gravies, simple coastal fries.
- **Oily fish**: vindalho-style, xacuti, roasted coconut masalas.
- **Venison**: xacuti, roasted coconut curries.
- **Wild boar**: Goan vindalho (perfect pork analogue), coconut-kokum braise.
- **Duck/goose**: vinegar-forward or xacuti-style curries.

## Pattern Blocks

- **Vindalho masala**: garlic, dried chilli, tamarind, vinegar → grind and marinate, then cook down with onions.
- **Xacuti base**: dry roast coconut and chillies with aromatics, grind, then fry and simmer with protein.
