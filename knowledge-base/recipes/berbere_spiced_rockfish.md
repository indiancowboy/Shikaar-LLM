# Berbere Spiced Rockfish

## Description
This one’s fast, flavorful, and built to impress.The key is the batter — punched up with berbere, an Ethiopian spice blend packed with dried chilies, garlic, ginger, paprika, and all kinds of warm, earthy depth.
I like to use rockfish I catch off the coast of San Diego, but any white, flaky fish like halibut, cod, or snapper, would taste great.



Ingredients
1/2 cup all-purpose flour
1 1/2 tablespoons berbere spice
1 (12-ounce) can cold beer 
Oil, for frying
4 pickle slices, finely chopped
4 tablespoons mayo

1 pound rockfish fillet, cut into portions


Preparation

Start by making your batter. In a mixing bowl, combine flour and 1 tablespoon of berbere spice. Slowly whisk in cold beer until a thick, smooth Think pancake batter with a little attitude.

Heat oil in a deep pan or wok to 350°F — just enough to submerge the fish.

While the oil heats, make the tartar sauce. Combine pickles, mayo, and ½ tablespoon of berbere in a small bowl. Mix well and set aside. Add a pinch of smoked shoyu salt if you’ve got it — it adds a rich, savory hit that works great with fried fish.

oat the fish pieces fully in the batter, then carefully slide them into the pan. Fry until golden and crispy, about 1–2 minutes depending on thickness.  145°F.

erve hot with a spoonful of berbere tartar sauce on the side.
