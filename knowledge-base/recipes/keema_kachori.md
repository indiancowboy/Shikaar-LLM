# Keema Kachori

## Description
If you’ve made the keema base from earlier, here’s one of the best ways to use it — stuffed into dough and deep-fried until golden. Kachori is one of my all-time favorite snacks. It’s simple but perfect: crispy dough on the outside, spicy, juicy filling on the inside.
There are a lot of variations — the most famous being pyaaz (onion) kachori I went with a keema version, using ground deer. You can swap in any ground meat you have on hand. The key is keeping the filling juicy enough to steam inside the dough while it fries.
Paired with a punchy curry leaf chutney, this version takes all the comfort of keema and wraps it into something you can eat with your hands — hot, crispy, and impossible to stop at just one.

Ingredients
For the Keema Filling:
1  ground game meat (deer, boar, venison, or beef)
½ cup yogurt

1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste

1 tablespoon salt
1 tablespoon deggi mirch (or other chili powder)
red onion, finely chopped
cup peas
cup diced eggplant
Oil for cooking

For the Curry Leaf Chutney:
1 cups fresh curry leaves
1 bunch cilantro
1 jalapeño (or more if you want it spicy)
cup shredded coconut
 cup water
Juice of 1 lime
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
Oil for tempering

For the Dough:
2 cups all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon salt
 cup oil
 cup water (plus more if needed)

For Frying:
Neutral oil (vocado oil works best)
Preparation
Prepare the Keema Filling:
In a bowl, mix the ground meat with yogurt, ginger garlic paste, salt, and deggi mirch. Let it marinate for at least hour.

Heat oil in a over medium-high. dd the onion and cook until translucent. Add the marinated and cook, stirring occasionally. dd peas and diced eggplant. Cook until the meat is just done but still juicy — don’t let it dry out. Add extra oil if needed, especially if using lean game meat. Let the filling cool completely before using.

Make the Curry Leaf Chutney:
In a blender, combine curry leaves, cilantro, jalapeño, coconut, water, and lime juice. Blend to your preferred consistency.

In a small pan, heat a little oil and toast cumin and mustard seeds until they pop. Stir the tempered seeds into the chutney. Set aside.

Prepare the Dough:
In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add oil and water, until you form a soft but slightly firm dough. Add more water if needed. Cover and let it rest for about 1 hour.

Assemble and Fry:
inch off enough dough to form a golf-ball-sized piece. Flatten it out, keeping the center slightly thicker than the edges. Add a spoonful of cooled keema filling. Carefully bring the edges together to enclose the filling, then gently flatten into a 3– round .

Heat oil to 285–315°F. Fry the kachoris slowly0–15 minutes  every 2–3 minutes once they start to floatboth sides are golden and crisp.

Drain on a rack or paper towels. Serve hot with curry leaf chutney.
