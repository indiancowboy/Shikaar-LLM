# Desi Deer Flatbread

## Description
This Indian-inspired flatbread recipe is a culinary adventure that transforms familiar ingredients into an exotic and flavorful experience. 

A harmonious blend of cauliflower, thinly sliced red onion, and ground venison forms the base, spiced with garam masala. The addition of melted cheese adds a creamy richness, while a drizzle of tangy yogurt and tamarind sauce brings a refreshing finish. 

Served on crispy, toasted naan and topped with ripe tomatoes and fresh cilantro, this dish is a delightful departure from the ordinary, perfect for those seeking a unique and satisfying meal.







Ingredients
Flatbread:
2 tablespoons butter
1 cup cauliflower, cut into small florets
1 red onion, thinly sliced
Salt, to taste
1 pound ground deer
3 tablespoons garam masala
1 cup shredded cheese (such as pepperjack)
4 naan
2 ripe tomatoes, diced
Fresh cilantro, chopped
Sauce:
1/2 cup plain yogurt
2 tablespoons tamarind paste

Preparation
Begin by heating a large pan or griddle over medium heat and melting 1 tablespoon of butter to coat the surface evenly. Add the cauliflower florets and red onion, sautéing them until they achieve a desirable golden hue and seasoning them generously with salt to enhance their flavor. 
Make a space in the center of the pan and add another tablespoon of butter. Next, add the ground venison, stirring and flipping frequently until the meat is browned and cooked through, about X minutes. 
Sprinkle the garam masala over the mixture, mixing well to ensure the spices evenly coat the ingredients and allow the flavors to meld together. Once the spices have incorporated fully, sprinkle the shredded cheese over the mixture and allow it to melt slightly.
Place the naan flatbreads and cook them until they are crispy and toasted to your liking. Once the naan is toasted, remove it from the pan and spread the venison and vegetable mixture evenly over each piece. 
Top each naan with additional cheese, sliced tomatoes, and chopped cilantro. 
In a small bowl, mix the plain yogurt with tamarind paste until well combined, creating a tangy sauce. Drizzle over the assembled flatbreads and serve.
