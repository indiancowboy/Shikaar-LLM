# Dungeness Crab Curry

## Description
The idea for this crab curry came after a trip to the coast, where I bought the freshest, sweetest crab straight off a dockside boat. I wanted to pair that sweetness with the bold, layered flavors I grew up eating—so I simmered the crab in a rich masala of garlic, ginger, chili, turmeric, and fresh green onions. Finished with a sprinkle of cilantro, it’s a dish that celebrates both the depth of Indian cooking and the simple joy of cooking with seafood at its peak.




Ingredients
2 tablespoons canola oil or avocado oil 
2-3 Dungeness crabs (cleaned)
6 cloves garlic
3-inch piece fresh ginger 
1 red or green chili 
1 tablespoon kosher salt
1 red onion
1/2 large tomato or 1 small 
2 tablespoons garam masala
1 tablespoon turmeric
1/4 cup diced green onion 
1 bunch cilantro leaves
Preparation
To begin, thinly slice the onion, dice the tomato, and smash and peel the garlic cloves. Peel and slice the ginger, and slice the chili. Place the chili, garlic, ginger, and 1 teaspoon of kosher salt into a mortar and pestle, and grind or mash until a paste forms (the salt will help to break down the ingredients). 

Clean the crabs by breaking them into halves and removing all legs. You should have 2 claws, 6 legs, and 2 body halves per crab. Make a few cracks in the legs and claws, this will allow the masale to penetrate the meat while cooking. 

Heat the oil in a 6- or 8-quart Dutch oven or large pot over medium heat for 1 minute. Add the onions and sauté until translucent, about 2-3 minutes. Incorporate the diced tomatoes and cook for an additional 3 minutes. Next, add the ginger, garlic, and chili paste and sauté for another minute. Reduce the heat to medium-low and stir in 1/2 cup of water, as well as the turmeric, and garam masala. Continue cooking for 4-5 minutes, allowing the water to mostly evaporate and the oil to begin separating from the vegetables and masala.

Add the cleaned crab to the pot, mixing well to coat it with the masala. Cook on medium-low heat for 3 minutes. Lower the heat to low, then add 2 cups of water, as well as the green onions, and 2 teaspoons of salt. Stir well, scraping the bottom of the pan. Cover and simmer over low heat for 25 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes. Once done, turn off the heat, stir the curry, and scatter fresh cilantro leaves over the top. Let rest for 15 minutes before stirring and serving.
