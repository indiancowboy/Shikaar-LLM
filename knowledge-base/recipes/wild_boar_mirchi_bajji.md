# Wild Boar Mirchi Bajji

## Description
This recipe is a flavorful twist on Mirchi Baji, a popular Indian street food made with green chilies dipped in a spiced chickpea flour batter and fried until crispy. Traditionally, Mirchi Bajji uses long green peppers like banana or bhavnagri chilies, but here, we’re elevating the dish by using Anaheim peppers and stuffing them with a savory boar sausage mixture. The result is a heartier and less spicy version of the classic snack, perfect as an appetizer or a fun side dish.








Ingredients
NAME:
Oil, for frying
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
8-10 curry leaves
1 pound ground boar (or boar sausage, seasoned with garam masala)
1 medium onion, finely chopped
2 tablespoons garam masala (if using plain ground boar)
Salt, to taste
6-8 large Anaheim chiles
Batter:
1 cup chickpea flour 
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
Tamarind Yogurt Dipping Sauce:
1/2 cup plain yogurt
1 tablespoon tamarind paste
1 teaspoon sugar
Salt, to taste
Fresh cilantro, for garnish
Preparation
Start by heating oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the mustard seeds and curry leaves, allowing them to crackle. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns golden and caramelized. Stir in the ground boar and cook, stirring often, until the meat is fully cooked, about 10-15 minutes. Set the boar mixture aside to cool.

Next, prepare the Anaheim chiles by making a lengthwise slit in each starting just below the top and stopping just before the tip. Gently open the peppers and carefully stuff them with the cooled boar mixture.

To make the batter, combine the chickpea flour, salt, and pepper in a shallow bowl. Gradually whisk in water until you form a thick, smooth batter that clings to the peppers without dripping (about 2 cups). Heat oil to 350°F in a deep pan. Dip each stuffed pepper into the batter, ensuring they’re fully coated, and fry until crispy and golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.

For the tamarind yogurt dipping sauce, mix the yogurt, tamarind paste, sugar, and salt until smooth. Adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve the fried stuffed peppers hot with the dipping sauce and garnish with fresh cilantro.
