# Braised Lingcod in Ginger Soy Broth

## Description
This recipe is a versatile and elegant way to highlight the delicate flavors of mild white fish such as lingcod, halibut, or rockfish. Poaching the fish in a fragrant broth of soy sauce, ginger, and green onions keeps it tender, moist, and deeply flavorful. It’s served over a bed of rice—but not just any rice—this one is gently simmered with cardamom, kaffir lime leaves, and whole peppercorns, infusing it with warm spice, citrusy perfume, and a gentle peppery bite. The final flourish is a pour of hot oil over fresh herbs, releasing their aroma in a fragrant sizzle that makes the whole dish come alive.




Ingredients
2 pounds Lingcod or Rockfish
Rice: 
1 cup rice
3 black cardamom pods
1 tablespoon peppercorns
4 kaffir lime leaves (see Note)
Broth: 
1/4 cup soy sauce
 piece ginger
1 bunchsliced green onions, white and green parts
2 tablespoons oil (such as vegetable or coconut)
Fresh cilantro, leaves and stems, for serving 
Pickled chilies, for serving
Preparation
Start by placing 1 cup of rice, 1 cup of water, cardamom, peppercorns, and kaffir lime leaves in an Instant Pot. Pressure cook on low for 12 minutes, allowing the device to naturally release for 10 minutes after cooking. 

Meanwhile, in a shallow pan, add just enough water to cover your fish and season it with soy sauce, ginger, and the whites of green onions. Bring this mixture to a light simmer over medium-low heat. Carefully add the fish and cook until its internal temperature reaches 140 degrees Fahrenheit. Transfer the fish and broth to a serving dish. Next, arrange the remaining green onions over the fish along with the cilantro. 

Next, add the oil to a small skillet and heat over high heat until it begins to smoke. Immediately remove the pan from the heat and carefully pour the hot oil over the fish. It will sizzle and may splatter so be cautious. The oil will infuse the herbs with its heat. For an optional touch, add pickled chilies over the fish for a tangy and spicy complement. Finally, serve the fish and its broth over the fragrant rice and enjoy the harmonious blend of flavors.

Note: Kaffir lime leaf can be purchased at any  grocery store or online.
