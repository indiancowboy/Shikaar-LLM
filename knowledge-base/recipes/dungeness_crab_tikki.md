# Dungeness Crab Tikki

## Description
This recipe takes the beloved Indian street-side aloo tikki and lifts it into something indulgent with the natural sweetness of Dungeness crab. Aloo tikki is famous for its chatpatta personality—spicy, sweet, salty, and tangy all at once—what Chef Gaggan Anand calls true Indian umami. The crab folds seamlessly into that flavor bomb, adding richness and depth while keeping the soul of the tikki unmistakably classic. If fresh Dungeness isn’t in season, good lump crab meat is a perfect stand-in.


Ingredients

Title: 
12 ounces Dungeness crab meat (or lump crab meat)
4 small potatoes, boiled and mashed
2 shallots, finely chopped
2 chilies, finely chopped (adjust to spice preference)
1 tablespoon curry powder
1 tablespoon salt
1 tablespoon turmeric
1 tablespoon tamarind paste
1/2 cup Aloo Bhujia (crushed, for coating)
Oil, for frying

Preparation
Make the sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together the hili , yogurt, tamarind paste, and lime juice until smooth. Transfer to a squeeze bottle if desired for easier serving.
In a large mixing bowl, combine the crab meat, mashed potatoes, shallots, chilies, curry powder, salt, turmeric, and tamarind paste. Mix until well combined, then shape into patties about 1/2-inch thick. 
Roll each patty in crushed aloo bhujia.
Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the patties for 2–3 minutes per side, until golden brown and crisp. Remove and drain briefly on paper towels.
Arrange the tikkis on a serving plate and drizzle generously with the prepared sauce. Serve hot, with extra sauce on the side if desired.
