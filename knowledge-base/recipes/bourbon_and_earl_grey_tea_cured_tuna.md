# Bourbon and Earl Grey Tea Cured Tuna

## Description
This recipe is a sophisticated and flavorful way to enjoy tuna. The tuna is first cured with a blend of salt, brown sugar, Earl Grey tea leaves, and bourbon, and togarashi, an all-purpose seasoning blend from Japan known for [description]. The cure imparts a balanced sweetness, aromatic depth, and a subtle kick of heat to the fish. 
Once cured, the tuna is paired with homemade tapioca crisps, which are created by cooking and dehydrating tapioca pearls mixed with squid ink and togarashi. The crisps are then fried to a perfect crunch. 
The final dish features diced cured tuna topped on the crisp, with optional garnishes like pickled chilies and chives. This elegant appetizer marries rich, complex flavors with a delightful texture, making it a standout choice for special occasions or an indulgent treat. 



Ingredients
Tuna Cure: 
200g non-iodized salt (see note)
70g brown sugar (see note)
2 tablespoons togarashi
2 packets Earl Grey tea, opened 
4 tablespoons bourbon of choice
Tuna:
1 pound of tuna fillets 
1 tablespoons soy sauce 
Tapioca Crisp: 
1/2 cup tapioca pearls 
1 tablespoon squid ink
1 tablespoon togarashi
Oil, for frying (I prefer Chosen Foods avocado oil) 
Preparation
In a medium bowl, combine the salt, brown sugar, togarashi, Earl Grey tea leaves, and bourbon, mixing until the texture resembles wet sand. Lay the tuna on a sheet of plastic wrap and generously coat it with the cure mixture, turn and coat the other side, ensuring even coverage. Wrap the tuna tightly in the plastic wrap.

Place the tuna in a tray to catch any moisture, then set a heavy plate or skillet on top and gently press down. Transfer to the refrigerator and allow the tuna to cure for 8-12 hours for optimal results. 

To make the tapioca crisps: Preheat the oven to 175°F. In a large saucepan, combine 2 1/2 cups water and the tapioca pearls. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat and cook until the pearls turn translucent, about X minutes. Once cooked, transfer the tapioca to a strainer and rinse thoroughly to remove excess starch. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Stir in the squid ink and togarashi until fully incorporated. Spread the mixture in a thin layer on the prepared baking tray and bake until fully dehydrated, 2-3 hours. 

Allow the tapioca to cool for 5-10 minutes. Meanwhile, line a plate with paper towels. Use your hands to break the tapioca into X-size pieces. Next, pour X inch of oil into a large skillet and heat over medium-high until 375°F (190°C). Fry the tapioca crisps one at a time, watching closely as they will puff up within seconds. Transfer finished crisps to the paper towel-lined plate.
Lastly, assemble the dish: Dice the cured tuna after rinsing off any remaining cure and add it to a large bowl. Add the soy sauce and toss until evenly coated. Place the seasoned tuna on top of the tapioca crisps. Feel free to garnish with your favorite toppings—I prefer pickled chilies and chives.

Note: I find the most consistent results when I use metric weights for this recipe. You’re welcome to estimate or use cups instead. 200g of salt is about 2/3 cup. For brown sugar, it varies based on if you’re using light or dark (dark is heavier, thus you need slightly less) and if you’re packing it or not. Overall, you’re looking at just a smidge under 1/3 cup if you’re using packed light brown sugar.
