# Keema (Ground Meat)

## Description
Keema — meaning “minced meat” — is a dish with deep roots across the Indian subcontinent. Introduced during the Mughal era, keema became a staple of royal kitchens, where it was often prepared with lamb, rich spices, and dried fruits. Over time, it made its way into everyday homes, becoming something far more humble and versatile — a spiced ground meat dish cooked with aromatics and often paired with peas to make the popular keema mattar.

Every food culture has its own version of ground meat comfort: burgers, meatballs, kebabs, areyes. For us, it’s keema — deeply spiced, endlessly adaptable, and perfect for whatever game you’ve got on hand. As hunters and anglers, we’re always working with ground venison, wild boar, elk, or bear. This is one of the best ways to turn those packs in the freezer into something bold, filling, and delicious.

This version gives you a base marinade for keema that keeps the meat juicy, tender, and full of flavor. Use it as a jumping-off point — adjust the spice, add onions, garlic, tomatoes, or throw in peas or potatoes depending on the dish you’re aiming for. 

Ingredients: 

1 lb ground game meat 
½ cup yogurt 
2 tablespoons garam masala 
1 tablespoon salt 

In a bowl, combine all the ingredients and mix well until the yogurt and spices are fully worked into the meat. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour — but if you can, give it 24. The yogurt helps tenderize the meat and locks in moisture.
