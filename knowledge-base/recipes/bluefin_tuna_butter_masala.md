# Bluefin Tuna Butter Masala

## Description
Butter chicken may be the gold standard of Indian comfort food, but this twist with fresh bluefin tuna brings a fresh coastal twist. The sauce is built on caramelized onion, garlic, ginger, and warm spices, rounded out with tomato, yogurt, and butter for that signature silky finish. Quick-seared peppers and tuna add freshness and depth, creating a dish that feels indulgent yet light. Served with rice or roti, it’s a catch-and-cook recipe that proves butter masala belongs far beyond the chicken pot.

Ingredients

1/2 onion, chopped
1 whole garlic bulb, peeled
1/4-inch piece fresh ginger, minced
1/2 tablespoon garam masala
2 teaspoons degi mirch (or Kashmiri chili powder)
1/2 cup plain yogurt
1/2 stick unsalted butter (4 tablespoons)
2 bell peppers, chopped
1/2 tablespoon turmeric
Salt, to taste
Fresh cilantro, for garnish
Preparation
Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. dd the onion and cook until translucent, tir in the ginger, garam masala, and degi mirch. Sauté until the mixture begins to caramelize and release its aroma. Add the tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their liquid, then stir in the yogurt and butter. Blend the mixture with an immersion blender until smooth, adding water if needed to achieve a silky sauce.
In a separate pan, heat a little oil over high heat. Add the bell peppers and sauté until just tender. Push them to the side of the pan, add the turmeric, then sear the tuna quickly until browned. Pour in the  and reduce the heat to low immer for 2–3 minutes until cooked through.
Finish with a scatter of fresh cilantro and serve hot with steamed rice or warm roti.
