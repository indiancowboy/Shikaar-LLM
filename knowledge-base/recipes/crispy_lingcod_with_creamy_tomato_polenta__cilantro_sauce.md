# Crispy Lingcod with Creamy Tomato Polenta & Cilantro Sauce

## Description
This dish is all about balance — crispy fish, creamy polenta, bright green sauce, and just enough heat. Lingcod has a mild, slightly sweet flavor that holds up beautifully when fried. The polenta adds richness and cilantro sauce brings a fresh, zesty kick that cuts through everything. It’s cozy, bold, and easy to riff on



Ingredients
Lingcod:
1 lingcod fillets
1 t salt
1 t MSG (optional)
Avocado oil, for shallow frying

Breading:
Panko breadcrumbs

2 eggs, whisked


Creamy Tomato Polenta:
1 cup polenta

1 cup milk
½ cup shredded cheddar cheese
1 white onion, finely chopped
1 bunch cilantro stems, finely chopped
2 medium tomatoes, pureed
4 cloves garlic, minced
Seasoning of choice

Cilantro Sauce:
⅓ cup mayo
Juice of 1 lemon
1 bunch cilantro leaves
1 jalapeño (or serrano/habanero for more heat)
4 cloves garlic, minced


Quick Pickled Jalapeños (optional):
1 jalapeño, thinly sliced
½ cup red wine vinegar

Preparation


Bread and Fry the Fish
Season  with salt and MSG. Let the rest while you prep your dredging station. 


Dip each fillet into the egg, then press into the panko. For extra crunch, double-dip: egg → panko → egg → panko again. Shallow fry in avocado oil over medium-high heat until golden and crispy on both sides. Internal temp should hit 145°F.

Make the Polenta Base
Bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Stir in the polenta and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring often, until thick.

Meanwhile, make the sofrito: In a skillet, sauté onion, garlic, and chopped cilantro stems in oil until soft. Add your seasoning and the pureed tomatoes. Cook until most of the moisture evaporates and the mixture thickens.

Stir milk and cheddar into the cooked polenta until smooth. Fold in the tomato sofrito and mix until fully incorporated.

Blend the Cilantro Sauce
In a jar or blender, combine mayo, lemon juice, cilantro leaves, jalapeño, and garlic. Blend until smooth with an immersion blender or standard blender. Thin with a splash of water or more lemon juice if needed.

Quick Pickle the Jalapeños (Optional)
Place thinly sliced jalapeños in a small jar or bowl. Cover with red wine vinegar and  30 minutes.

To Plate
Spoon a generous layer of creamy tomato polenta onto each plate. Top with a crispy lingcod fillet. Drizzle with cilantro sauce and add a few pickled jalapeños if you want that extra bite.
