# Ponzu Ginger Goose

## Description
This goose recipe is inspired by one of my favorite Chinese takeout dishes, Mongolian Beef. 
The goose breasts are first salted and rested, then sliced and seasoned before being briefly fried to achieve a crispy exterior while remaining tender inside. The accompanying sauce, a harmonious blend of caramelized onion, aromatic ginger and garlic, and a balance of Worcestershire and Ponzu, is enhanced with a touch of brown sugar for depth and richness. Finished with a brief simmer, this dish is perfect served over rice, with a sprinkle of furikake and a kick of pickled chilies. 





Ingredients
2 goose breast 
Salt, to taste
1 tablespoon pepper 
1/2 cup cornstarch 
1/2 cup all-purpose flour 
1 large egg
Avocado oil 
1 onion 
1/2-inch piece fresh ginger 
4 garlic cloves
1/2 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
2 tablespoons Ponzu Sauce 
1/2 tablespoon brown sugar
Furikake, for serving
Pickled chilies, for serving
Preparation
Start by salting the goose breasts and allowing them to rest in the refrigerator for 3-5 hours. Rinse off the salt and slice the breasts into thin strips, about 1/8-inch. Toss the strips with a tablespoon of freshly cracked pepper.

In a shallow bowl, thoroughly combine the cornstarch and flour. In another shallow bowl, beat the egg, add the goose strips, and toss to coat evenly. Dredge the coated strips in the cornstarch-flour mixture, shaking off any excess flour.  

Heat the avocado oil in a pan and fry the goose strips for one minute on each side, aiming for a crispy exterior while leaving the inside slightly undercooked; the goose will finish cooking in the sauce.

For the sauce, heat 1 tablespoon of avocado oil in a pan and sauté the onion until it begins to caramelize. Add the ginger and garlic, cooking them down until fragrant, about 1 minute. Then, incorporate the Worcestershire sauce and Ponzu sauce and cook, stirring often, until reduced by half, about 10 minutes. Stir in the brown sugar and let it dissolve completely.

To assemble, add the goose strips to the sauce, ensuring each piece is well coated. Simmer over low heat for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve over rice, garnished with furikake and pickled chilies.
