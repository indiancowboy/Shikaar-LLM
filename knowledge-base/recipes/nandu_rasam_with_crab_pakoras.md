# Nandu Rasam with Crab Pakoras

## Description
This dish is a marriage of two coastal traditions—South Indian nandu rasam (crab rasam) and b bisque. The rasam brings spice, tang, and heat, while the bisque technique lends depth and silkiness. To complete the experience, crispy crab pakoras float on top, adding both crunch and indulgence. It’s comfort food with elegance, a dish that nods to heritage while embracing innovation.

This dish marries South Indian nandu rasam (crab rasam) with French-inspired crab bisque. The rasam brings spice, tang, and heat, while the bisque technique—slowly reducing crab shells and aromatics—adds depth and silkiness. Crispy crab pakoras float on top for crunch and indulgence.

Pakoras are Indian spiced fritters made by coating ingredients in seasoned chickpea flour batter and deep-frying until golden. Traditionally a street food or appetizer featuring vegetables, here we use crab meat for an indulgent twist. The challenge? Keeping them crispy in soup. The carbonated batter creates an extra-light, airy texture that helps these morsels maintain their crunch even as they soak up the spiced broth. It's comfort food with elegance, bridging continents and techniques.

Ingredients
Crab & Pakoras:
1 large Dungeness crab (about 23 pounds)
1 cup chickpea flour (for batter), plus 
2 tablespoons (for binding the meat)
2 green onions, finely sliced
 teaspoon garam masala, divided
Salt, to taste

½ cup lub soda (or carbonated beverage of choice)
Oil, for frying
Rasam:
2 tablespoons Avocado oil
1 red onion, finely chopped
4 garlic cloves, minced
1-inch piece ginger, peeled and minced
1/2 cup tomato puree
2 teaspoons garam masala
1 cinnamon stick
3 whole star anise
810 curry leaves, plus more for garnish
Salt, to taste
1/4 cup heavy cream
To serve: 
Cilantro, optional

Preparation

Start by boiling the crab in a large pot of salted water until fully cooked, about 10–12 minutes. Reserve the cooking liquid. Once cool enough to handle, pick out all the crab meat and set it aside. Do not discard the shells. 

To make the pakoras, combine the crab meat with 2 tablespoons of the chickpea flour, green onions, 1 teaspoon of the garam masala, and the salt. Use your hands to orm small bite-sized balls. In another bowl, whisk together the remaining 1 cup of chickpea flour, garam masala, and just enough club soda to create a thick batter. of oil in a large skillet over medium-high until 350°F (175°C)Dip the crab balls into the batter and fry until golden and crisp. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate and set aside. 

For the rasam, heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in tomato puree, garam masala, cinnamon, star anise, curry leaves, and salt. Cook for 5 minutes dd the reserved crab cooking liquid and immer for 20 minutes.

Add 2 cups of the tomato mixture to a blender along with the reserved crab shells. Blend on medium until smooth Line a fine mesh sieve with cheesecloth and place over the pot with the tomato mixture. Strain the crab shell mixture back into the pot. Discard any solids. Stir in the heavy cream and adjust to taste. The broth should now have the body of a bisque with the spice and aromatics of a rasam.

To serve, place a few hot crab pakoras into each bowl. Ladle the steaming rasam over the top and garnish with fresh curry leaves or cilantro. The pakoras soak up the spiced broth while keeping their crispy edges, making each spoonful both crunchy and soothing.
