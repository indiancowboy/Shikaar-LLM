# Fish Curry with Okra and Green Beans

## Description
This curry balances smoky grilled fish, roasted tomatoes, and spiced vegetables for a dish that feels both rustic and refined. The fish is marinated in yogurt, turmeric, and curry leaves, then grilled until charred and tender. Green beans simmer in a fragrant curry base while crispy fried okra is folded in at the end, adding texture and depth. Finished with fresh cilantro, it’s a vibrant and comforting dish that showcases Indian flavors at their best.

Ingredients


1/2 cup plain yogurt

1 teaspoon turmeric

8–10 curry leaves
Juice of 1 lime

4 tomatoes
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 onion, finely chopped
1-inch piece ginger, minced
4 garlic cloves, minced
10 curry leaves (for curry base)
1 teaspoon turmeric
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon degi mirch (or Kashmiri chili powder)
Salt, to taste
1/2 pound green beans, trimmed
1/2 pound okra, sliced
Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish

Preparation

In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, turmeric, curry leaves, and lime juice.  fish fillets and marinate for at least 2 hours or up to 24 hours .

Preheat a grill to medium-high. Grill the fish until lightly charred and just cooked through, about 3–4 minutes per side. Place the tomatoes on the grill alongside the fish and roast until blistered. Remove and set aside.

For the curry base, heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds and let them pop, then stir in the onion, ginger, garlic, curry leaves, turmeric, garam masala, degi mirch, and salt. Cook until fragrant, about 5 minutes. Add the roasted tomatoes and green beans, along with a splash of water, and simmer until the beans are tender and the curry develops a rich, saucy consistency.

Meanwhile, fry the okra in hot oil until golden and crisp. Season lightly with salt.

To serve, add the grilled fish and fried okra to the curry base, folding gently to combine. Garnish with fresh cilantro and enjoy with warm roti, naan, or steamed rice.
